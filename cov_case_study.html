Overview

Vancouver encourages passive cooling.
Its own website makes it nearly impossible.

The City of Vancouver actively promotes passive cooling — shade structures, tree planting, and other low-impact interventions to reduce urban heat. But the permit process required to implement them is fragmented, buried in technical language, and routed through multiple departments. Community organizations face wait times of 3–6 months just to get approval for straightforward, low-risk projects.

The Brief
Design a solution that helps community organizations navigate the City of Vancouver's permit process for passive cooling projects — reducing friction, wait times, and confusion.
The Solution
A new pre-approved permit feature integrated directly into the existing City of Vancouver website — allowing organizations to bypass the full permit process for pre-vetted, low-risk passive cooling projects.

The Challenge

Community organizations couldn't determine
what was even allowed.

Through research, we identified that the core problem wasn't just slow bureaucracy — it was a fundamental lack of clarity at every stage of the process. Organizations couldn't determine what was permitted, who needed to approve it, or which solutions the City would accept.

Research Question
What barriers do community-serving organizations encounter when implementing small-scale, low-risk passive cooling projects on properties they own in Vancouver?
Current permitting journey — 5 phases, months of wait time, and friction at every step
Prototype Walkthrough

A new feature embedded into
the existing City of Vancouver website.

Rather than building a separate platform, the solution integrates directly into vancouver.ca — meeting users where they already go. The prototype demonstrates three key steps: discovering the feature, checking pre-approval status by address, and accessing project-specific guidance.

Interactive prototype — built and prototyped in Figma
01
Entry Point
A new button on the City of Vancouver homepage

A clear entry point added to the City homepage allows users to quickly check whether their passive cooling project requires a permit — simplifying the discovery phase immediately.

Research Insight
The website contains dense information spread across multiple pages, making it difficult to quickly understand what applies to a specific project.
02
Permit Discovery
Check pre-approval status by address

The pre-approved cooling permits page allows users to enter their address and instantly see which passive cooling projects are already approved for their specific location — no enquiry submission required.

Research Insight
When requirements are unclear, applicants must submit a Development Permit enquiry or consult City staff — making it difficult to determine whether regulations apply before starting an application.
03
Project Details
Location-specific guidance, clearly explained

After entering an address, the system displays the relevant cooling project and clearly states whether a permit is required — with plain-language explanations of why, and what the next steps are.

Research Insight
Urban planning regulations depend on property zoning, which varies by location. Permit requirements therefore depend on the specific site of the project.
Design Process

Building the feature inside
the City of Vancouver's existing UI system.

As the sole designer, I was responsible for building all UI components in Figma — replicating and extending the City of Vancouver's existing design language to ensure the new feature felt native to the platform. This included navigation elements, directory components, map interfaces, and location information panels.
